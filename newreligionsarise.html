New Religions Arise

During the development of Hinduism, new religious movements emerged, such as Jainism, Buddhism, and Sikhism. These religions challenged the established Brahmanical order.

Jainism and Buddhism

Jainism, founded by Mahavira, and Buddhism, founded by Siddhartha Gautama (the Buddha), both emphasized non-violence and renunciation, gaining widespread followers in India.
